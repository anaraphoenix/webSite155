  • Loco Moco

One summer evening, inspired by a memorable trip to Hawaii, I decided to recreate a dish that had captured my heart: Loco Moco. In my kitchen, the aroma of cooking rice filled the air, setting the foundation for this comforting meal. As the rice simmered, I carefully formed ground beef patties, envisioning the lush landscapes of the islands. The sizzle of the patties hitting the hot pan was like music, promising a delicious outcome. Once cooked to perfection, the patties were placed atop the fluffy bed of rice. The final, crowning glory was a fried egg, gently laid over the beef, its yolk gleaming like a golden sunset. As I poured the rich, homemade gravy over it all, the kitchen seemed to embrace me with warmth and the spirit of aloha. It was more than just a meal; it was a cherished moment, a connection to a place I had left behind but could revisit with every bite.

Ingredients

  • 1lb of ground beef
  • 1lb of hot italian sausage
  • 2 cups of rice
  • 1 package of mushroom gravy
  • eggs (make 1 to 2 fried eggs per plate) + 1 for binder

Directions

  • Mix the pork and the beef and crack an egg in it for a binder. Mix together well.
  • Cook rice preferred method. I just use a rice cooker.
  • Press meat mixture into patties about 1/4 to 1/2lb each and cook.
  • Make mushroom gravy.
  • Lastly fry eggs making sure yokes stay runny.
  • The important thing is plating this dish. In a bowl place a couple scoops of rice then place a patty in the middle, if the patties are on the smaller side you can use two. Then pour the gravy over the patties. Place one or two eggs on top.
