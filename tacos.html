Embarking on my quest to make chicken tacos for the first time, I was brimming with confidence, armed with a recipe I barely understood. I marinated the chicken with a hodgepodge of spices, convinced I was on the brink of culinary greatness. As the chicken sizzled in the pan, my aim was for it to be a little charred but not burnt, seeking that perfect balance of smoky flavor and tender juiciness. However, I underestimated the spice level, transforming my kitchen into a tear gas chamber. Amidst coughing fits and frantic ventilation attempts, I managed to assemble the tacos using store-bought tortillas, which were a safe haven in my kitchen chaos. The end result was a hilariously spicy concoction that required gallons of water to down. That night, I learned that "a pinch" of cayenne pepper means something vastly different across recipes, and my quest for chicken tacos became a fiery tale of survival and laughter with every tear-inducing bite, celebrating the perfectly charred yet not burnt chicken that was the heart of the dish.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1/2 cup BBQ sauce
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 1/2 tsp garlic power
  • Flour tortillas, "street taco" size
  • 2lbs of boneless skinless chicken thighs
  • 1 small red onion
  • 6 strips of bacon
  • 8oz of colby jack or monterrey jack cheese, shredded
  • Chopped cilantro (enough for garnish)
  • Crema, for drissle. I make Uncle Dan's Ranch Dip
  • *optional, pickled jalapenos

Directions

  • Preheat oven to 350°
  • Mix oil, honey, BBQ, paprika and garlic powder.
  • Cut chicken into 1/4 inch pieces.
  • Put chicken in a bowl or ziplock and pour spice mixture over to marinate place in fridge for at least 15min.
  • In the meantime, dice onion, shred cheese and chop cilantro.
  • Use kitchen shears to cut bacon into bacon bite size pieces and cook until desired crispiness
  • In same pan the has the bacon grease, heat pan to med-high and cook chicken until cooked and there is some char.
  • On a flat baking sheet, place parchment paper. Lay out tortillas overlapping slightly.
  • Place cooked chicken on top of the tortillas. Then sprinkle bacon, cheese and onion. Add jalapeno if desired.
  • Bake for 15min.
  • Drissle on crema or Uncle Dan's and top with cilantro.
  • Place entire tray on the table and everyone can pull and share!
